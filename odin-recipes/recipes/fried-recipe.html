Fried Rice

About Fried Rice

Fried rice is a popular and versatile dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with oil, vegetables, eggs, and seasonings like soy sauce or spices, often combined with proteins such as chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu.

Originating from Asia and enjoyed worldwide, it is valued for its quick preparation, ability to use leftover rice, and adaptability to local flavorsâ€”ranging from Chinese and Thai versions to African styles with bold spices. Whether served as a main dish or a side, fried rice remains a flavorful comfort food that blends creativity and culture in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of white rice
  • ½ cup frozen green peas
  • ⅔ cup chopped baby carrots
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil

Instruction

  1. Combine 4 cups of water and 2 cups of white rice in a saucepan, bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat, then cover and simmer until the rice is tender and the water has been absorbed.
  3. Remove from head and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Place carrots and green peas in a small saucepan and cover with water. Boil for a minute and drain it in a colander
  5. Heat a saucepan over heat. Pour in vegetable oil, then stir in carrots, peas and garlic — cook for about 30 seconds.
  6. Add eggs and stir quickly to scramble eggs with vegetables.
  7. Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy.
